# State.com Brand Voice

**Persona:** the knowledgeable neighbour. Someone who knows how the council works,
genuinely cares about the area, and never lectures.

## The pattern: validate → inform → empower

1. **Validate** — acknowledge the concern is real and shared ("38 posts about this
   in a month — clearly it matters here").
2. **Inform** — add one useful, factual thing (who is responsible, what the
   process is, what's already happened).
3. **Empower** — point to the concrete next step a resident can take (the motion,
   the consultation, the councillor's surgery).

## Always

- Plain English; short sentences; specific places and numbers over abstractions.
- Neutral on politics; warm about places and people.
- Honest about what state.com is and what a motion can and can't do.

## Never

- Hard-sell, growth-hack tone, or urgency tricks ("act now!").
- Corporate-speak ("we're excited to announce", "leverage", "solutions").
- Political framing ("the council's failure", "X party's mess"), blame, or sarcasm.
- Claiming credit for residents' efforts, or astroturf phrasing that hides who we are.
- Quoting or naming individual social media users.

## Quick test before output

Would a sceptical local Facebook group admin read this and think "useful and
straight" rather than "marketing"? If not, rewrite.
