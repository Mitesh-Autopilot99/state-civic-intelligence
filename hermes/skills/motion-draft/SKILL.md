# Motion Drafting

A motion is a verified-constituent ask to a named MP or councillor. Draft 2–3 per
week maximum, only for items with suggested_action = seed_motion (or when Mitesh
asks). Load state-brand-voice and state-compliance alongside this skill.

## Format

**Title** (≤ 12 words, the specific ask): "Fix the pedestrian crossing on London Road, Croydon"

**To:** [MP name, constituency] or [Ward councillor if the issue is council-level —
say which and why; council issues: bins, potholes, planning, parks, ASB enforcement;
MP issues: NHS access, national policy, transport infrastructure]

**The issue** (2–3 sentences): what, where exactly, who it affects. Use the
aggregate evidence: "Residents have raised this repeatedly — [volume] separate
reports in the past week with strong local engagement."

**The ask** (1–2 sentences): one specific, achievable action with a timescale.
Not "do something about traffic" but "commission a safety review of the junction
by end of Q3".

**Why it matters** (1–2 sentences): concrete local impact. Neutral framing.

Total: 120–220 words. No author references, no quotes from posts, no political blame.

## Process

1. Read the issue item (category, area, constituency, MP, summary, volume, engagement).
2. Decide MP vs councillor; if councillor, note that the ward must be confirmed.
3. Draft. 4. Present to Mitesh with: the draft, the evidence line, and a one-line
   note on anything to verify before creating it on state.com.
5. Ideal path (note it in your handoff): recruit a genuinely affected local as the
   named creator rather than seeding it ourselves.

## Verification
The draft is done when Mitesh replies approve/edit/reject. Log nothing personal.
