# Webcast caption/transcript permission requests (Tier 2)

**Why these exist:** YouTube's ToS only allows caption download by the video
*owner*, and Public-i/Civico expose no public transcript API
(research_tier2_tier3.md §6). The clean route is written permission from each
council — they own the recordings. webcast_source.py gets built only for
councils that say yes.

**How to use:** send each from your own email. Find the democratic services
address on each council's "contact democratic services" / committee services
page (typically of the form below — verify before sending). Keep replies;
a "yes" email is the permission record.

**Do not automate sending. Drafts only.**

---

## Template (identical body, per-council greeting/platform line)

**Subject:** Permission request — captions/transcripts of public meeting webcasts

Dear Democratic Services team,

I run a small civic-analysis project covering [BOROUGH] that produces a daily
internal summary of local issues residents are raising — planning, housing,
roads, waste and similar — so they can be raised with the right councillors
and MPs.

I'd like to include the public business of [COUNCIL]'s committee meetings.
Rather than scrape your webcasts, I'm writing to ask permission to retrieve
the captions/auto-transcripts of your **public** meeting recordings on
[PLATFORM], for aggregate analysis only.

To be clear about how the material would be used:

- Aggregate analysis only: topics, areas and volumes. No transcript is ever
  republished, quoted, or stored after analysis.
- Speakers are never identified: no councillor, officer or member of the
  public is named in any output.
- Public meetings only — nothing from restricted/exempt sessions.
- The output links back to your meeting pages, which may bring residents to
  your published agendas and webcasts.

If there's a preferred route (e.g. transcripts on request, or a bulk caption
export), I'm happy to work with whatever is easiest for your team. And if
this needs a formal request under a different process, please point me to it.

Thank you for your time,
Mitesh Thaker
thakermitesh89@gmail.com
